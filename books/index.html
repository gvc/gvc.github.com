The Friendly Orange Glow

4/5

A great story about a different "internet", before The Internet. The PLATO project started as an automatic teaching platform but, by crafting and encouraging a community of young and curious developers to stretch the limits of the system. This was done in the 60s and 70s and using a mainframe! Definitely well worth a look at this book if you're interested in the history of computing and the internet. It's also very interesting how Douglas Engelbart and Alan Kay's names tend to be brought up when we talk about the beginning of personal computing.

The Disappearance of Childhood

4/5

Picked this book after going on an Alan Kay binge and coming across this list of book recommendations. The list was mentioned by Alan Kay himself in a quora answer. The book itself proposes an interesting idea that childhood appeared when there was a separating factor between the adult world and the child's world. To prove this point he mentions the dark ages, when children (7+ years) were sent off to apprentice. When Gutemberg invented the printing press this changed. The world was divided between those who could read and those who could not. And when that happened, we could keep 'secrets' and the ugly face of society from kids. After setting this picture up he moves to how television and its visual, instead of intelectual, form of consumption is erasing this distinction.
